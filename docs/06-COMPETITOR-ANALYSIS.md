## THE GRAVEYARD: COMPANIES THAT TRIED AND FAILED

### 1. FIRSTV1SION (Spain, 2013-2022)
**What they built:** Single wearable camera embedded in basketball/football jerseys
**Investors:** Telefonica Wayra (€60K), Andres Iniesta (FC Barcelona), Serge Ibaka (NBA)
**Peak moment:** Broadcast in 12 countries during EuroLeague 2015 Real Madrid match

**Why They Failed:**
1. **Single-angle limitation** - One camera per player = boring POV, no multi-angle storytelling
2. **No biometric value prop** - Promised HR data but never consistently delivered it on broadcasts
3. **B2B dependency** - Relied on league partnerships that were slow to renew
4. **Hardware iteration speed** - Spanish hardware startup = slow chipset access, no supply chain depth
5. **No platform** - Just a hardware company. Gave footage to broadcasters with no recurring SaaS revenue
6. **Athlete comfort ignored** - Shirt was a separate smart garment, not compatible with team jerseys
7. **Battery life** - <60 min on a single charge; didn't cover full matches

**AthleteView Fix:** Patch Cam multi-angle system + biometric overlay on broadcast + own platform + jersey-universal design + 90 min battery

---

### 2. INTEL TRUE VIEW / REPLAY TECHNOLOGIES (USA, acquired 2016, shut 2021)
**What they built:** 5K free-viewpoint video using 38 fixed cameras around stadium perimeter
**Intel paid:** $175 million (2016 acquisition)
**Deployed:** 18 NFL stadiums, NFL Super Bowl, Wimbledon

**Why They Failed:**
1. **$2-6M per venue installation** - Too expensive to scale beyond elite US sports
2. **Zero wearable component** - Fixed cameras can't follow athlete movement intimately
3. **Intel strategic mismatch** - Intel's core business is chips (IDM 2.0). Sports broadcast is not a chip business
4. **No recurring revenue model** - Charged per-game licensing, not SaaS
5. **Shut down August 2021** - Intel dissolved the unit entirely during COVID-era restructuring
6. **Latency problem** - 3D reconstruction took minutes, not seconds. Not live-compatible

**AthleteView Fix:** Wearable = athletes bring their own cameras. Edge server 3DGS = real-time. $8L per team kit vs $2-6M per venue.

---

### 3. PEBBLE SMART WATCH / TOMTOM SPORTS (Wearable era 2015-2017)
**Failure pattern:** Accurate sensors + ugly design + poor UX + no outcome loop = abandonment

**Why They Failed:**
1. **Data without insight** - Showed HR numbers but didn't tell athletes what to DO with them
2. **Hardware-only business** - No recurring revenue after device sale
3. **No community / platform** - Data stayed on device, not shared
4. **Fitness band fatigue** - Users wore for 2 weeks then stopped

**AthleteView Fix:** Biometrics are for coaches + broadcasters (not just athletes). Data feeds team strategy dashboards AND fan experience.

### 4. NARRATIVE CLIP (Sweden, 2012-2017)
**What they built:** Life-logging wearable camera
**Raised:** $11M+ equity
**Failure:** Life-logging never became mainstream. No clear use case. B2C pivot failed.

**AthleteView Fix:** Narrow vertical (professional/semi-pro sports) with clear B2B buyers (leagues, teams, broadcasters)

### 5. HUMANE AI PIN (USA, 2024)
**Raised:** $230M
**Why Failed:** Ignored the phone that was already in everyone's pocket. Tried to replace too much at once.

**AthleteView Fix:** We AUGMENT existing broadcast infrastructure. We are an ADD-ON to current TV production, not a replacement.

## THE PATTERN OF FAILURE - COMMON THREADS

1. **Hardware-only revenue** = one-time sale, no recurring LTV
2. **B2B without SaaS** = dependent on slow enterprise sales cycles
3. **No platform play** = data goes to broadcaster, not into a proprietary ecosystem
4. **League/broadcaster dependency** = single point of failure
5. **Athlete comfort not solved** = teams rejected the hardware
6. **Too expensive to scale** = high BOM killed mass adoption
7. **Tried to replace TV** = should have augmented it

## ATHLETEVIEW'S STRUCTURAL MOAT

1. **Technology moat**: Multi-patch camera + 3DGS is the ONLY combination that exists
2. **Data moat**: First to have live biometric + video + spatial data per athlete at scale
3. **Platform moat**: Own streaming = own fan relationship = subscription revenue
4. **India moat**: IPL, ISL, PKL, Pro Kabaddi = 400M+ viewers = $0 customer acquisition from day 1
5. **Cost moat**: India manufacturing = 60% cheaper than Western competitors
6. **IP moat**: File patents on the Patch-to-MainCam BLE relay architecture + biometric overlay broadcast format
